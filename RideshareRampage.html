Rideshare Rampage

Rideshare Rampage was a case study of Crazy Taxi that I did with Wolverinesoft, Michigan's game development club.

Rideshare Rampage, made with Unreal Engine 5, is a homage to the classic arcade game Crazy Taxi. This high-energy driving game puts players behind the wheel as a rideshare driver racing against the clock to pick up and drop off passengers in a bustling urban environment. With its frenetic gameplay and colorful, chaotic cityscape, Rideshare Rampage delivers a nostalgic yet fresh gaming experience. Key contributions and implemented features include:

  • Developed NPC AI for pedestrians and other drivers, creating a dynamic and reactive city environment
  • Implemented core game logic, including passenger pickup/drop-off mechanics and scoring system
  • Assisted in designing and balancing the game's difficulty curve to ensure an engaging player experience

Working on Rideshare Rampage provided useful experience in collaborative game development. As part of a diverse team, I learned the importance of clear communication and effective task delegation. Coordinating my AI and game logic work with other team members' contributions taught me valuable lessons in code integration and version control. This project highlighted the power of brainstorming sessions and constructive feedback loops in refining game mechanics and features. Additionally, I gained insight into the dynamics of cross-functional teamwork, collaborating closely with artists, designers, and other programmers to ensure a cohesive final product.
